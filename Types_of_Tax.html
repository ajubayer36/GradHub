Types of Taxes

  • EARN
  • BUY
  • OWN

EARN

Earnings-related taxes are crucial for funding various government programs and services.

  • Individual Income Taxes: Taxes on personal income from sources like employment, investments, and more.
  • Corporate Income Taxes: Taxes imposed on business profits.
  • Payroll Taxes: Taxes collected from employee salaries and employer contributions, often supporting programs like Social Security and Medicare.
  • Capital Gains Taxes: Taxes on profits from asset sales, with distinctions between short-term and long-term gains.

BUY

Taxes on purchases play a significant role in government revenue and regulation.

  • Sales Taxes: Levied on the purchase of goods and services.
  • Gross Receipts Taxes: Taxes based on total business revenue, varying by location and business type.
  • Value-Added Taxes: Taxes applied at each stage of production and distribution, common in many countries.
  • Excise Taxes: Specific taxes on goods like alcohol, tobacco, gasoline, and luxury items.

OWN

Taxes related to ownership can include property and wealth taxes, as well as inheritance and tangible property taxes.

  • Property Taxes: Taxes on the value of real estate or immovable property, funding local government services.
  • Tangible Personal Property Taxes: Taxes on movable assets, such as equipment, machinery, and vehicles.
  • Estate and Inheritance Taxes: Taxes imposed on the transfer of assets from a deceased person to their heirs.
  • Wealth Taxes: Taxes on the net worth or total assets of individuals or households, addressing wealth inequality.
